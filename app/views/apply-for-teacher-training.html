Apply for teacher training: step by step

Check if you're eligible to apply for teacher training, how to find a course and how to apply.

  1. Step 1 Check if you're eligible to apply for teacher training

    To be eligible for teacher training, you need these qualifications (or their non-UK equivalents):

    • a degree
    • grade 4 (C) or above in English and maths GCSEs
    • grade 4 (C) or above in GCSE science if you want to teach primary

    If you do not have a degree you can teach in further education (age 14 to adult), or study for a degree leading to qualified teacher status. Learn more about eligibility for teacher training.

    If you're unsure about your eligibility, you can get support from an expert adviser.

  2. Step 2 Find a teacher training course

    Postgraduate teacher training courses are available across England, led by universities or schools. The course you choose will depend on your subject, degree class and location.

    1. Find postgraduate teacher training courses
    2. Get advice on choosing the right course

  3. Step 3 Apply

    Once you have found a teacher training course you can start your application.

    Depending on the course you select, you may have an option to apply through a new GOV.UK service called 'Apply for teacher training' or through UCAS. The Apply for teacher training service will eventually replace UCAS.

    As part of your application you will need to write a personal statement and arrange references.

    1. Preparing your personal statement
    2. Arranging your references

    You should get a decision within 40 days from when you apply.

  4. Step 4 Attend an interview

    After you have submitted your application you may be asked to attend an interview with the school or university providing the training.

    1. Preparing for your interview
